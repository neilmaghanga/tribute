Dr. Wangari Maathai

The Nobel Laureate

Kenyan social, environmental, and political activist and the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize

Here is a timeline of Dr.Wangari Maathai's life:

  • 1940 - Born in the village of Ihithe, Nyeri District, in the central highlands of Kenya.
  • 1959 - Graduated high school from .
  • 1964 - Obtained a Bachelor of Science in Biology from Mount St. Scholastica College in Kansas, US
  • 1966 - Completed a Master's of Science degree at the University of Pittsburgh, US
  • 1971 - She received her Ph.D. in veterinary anatomy at the University of Nairobi, becoming the 1st woman in East and Central Africa to achieve a Ph.D.
  • 1971 to 1977 - She worked with the National Council of Women of Kenya (NCWK), educating women in rural areas about environmental and social conditions affecting poor, rural people, especially women
  • 1977 - She established the Green Belt Movement, a non-profit grassroots NGO based in Kenya
  • 1989 - After hearing about plans to construct a 60-story skyscraper in Nairobi’s Uhuru Park, she wrote letters of protest to government officials, the media and the Ministry for Environment. The government ignored her inquiries and protests, and instead told the media she was “a crazy woman”
    Because of her work with the Green Belt Movement, she constantly faced opposition from the government. During a speech by the President later that year, he suggested that she be a proper woman in the African tradition and “respect men and be quiet”
  • 1998 - While attempting to plant a tree in Karura Forest, in protest of the privatization and destruction of the forest, she and her fellow protesters were attacked by a large group of men guarding the area
  • 2002 - In the elections, she was elected as a Member of Parliament
  • 2003 - She published “The Green Belt Movement: Sharing the Approach and the Experience,” the first of four books she wrote
  • 2004 - She became the 1st African woman and 1st environmentalist to receive the Nobel Peace Prize, for her outstanding work on sustainable development, democracy and peace
  • 2005 - Maathai was elected the first president of the African Union's Economic, Social and Cultural Council and was appointed a goodwill ambassador for an initiative aimed at protecting the Congo Basin Forest Ecosystem.
  • 2006 - She spearheaded the United Nations Billion Tree Campaign.
  • 2009 - Published "The Challenge for Africa" with her insights into the strengths and weaknesses of governance in Africa, her own experiences, and the centrality of environmental protection to Africa's future.
  • 2009 - Named as one of PeaceByPeace.com's first peace heroes.
  • 2011 - Wangari Maathai passed away on September 25th
“Finally I was able to see that if I had a contribution I wanted to make, I must do it, despite what others said. That I was OK the way I was. That it was all right to be strong.” - Wangari Maathai
